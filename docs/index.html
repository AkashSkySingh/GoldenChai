Ingredients

Ingredients can be found in most grocery stores, although some may only be available via specialty super markets. For first time drinkers, acquire smallest quantity of herbs and spices available as you will need fairly little per serving.

  • Ground Cinnamon
  • Assam Tea leaves
  • Fennel Seeds: "Sonf"
  • Carom Seeds: "Ajwain"
  • Green Cardamom: "Hari Lachis"
  • Black Cardamom: "Kali Lachis"
  • Whole Milk (or Substitute)
  • Sugar (or "Jaggery")
  • Water

Sweet Golden Chai

Description

A homemade, milk tea concoction based off of the Indian classic. Can be altered to personal taste palette. Intended for those individuals on the move, needing a taste of home. Recipe allows for the addition of ingredients, such as:

  • Ginger
  • Garlic
  • Black Pepper
  • Star Anise
As well as other herbs and spices.

Directions

You will need a grinder, a pot, a small strainer, and two mugs.The herbs and spices can be ground ahead of time and stored so making a cup of Chai can be done in a matter of minutes. This will avoid the necessity of grinding beforehand, unless you just enjoy the delicious aroma of the ingredients. Please read directions ahead of time as caution is necessary for later steps involving brewing.

  1. Place the pot on stove at medium heat and add 2 cups of water.

  2. Grind and all the following items into a pot.
    • 5 to 6 greed cardamoms
    • 1 to 2 black cardamoms
    • 1 teaspoon of Fennel Seeds
    • 1 teaspoon of Carom Seeds
    • 1 to 2 Tablespoons of Assam Tea leaves

  3. Add 1/2 teaspoon of Ground Cinnamon to the pot

  4. Add preferred amount of Sugar, or Jaggery. For a semi-sweet cup, I prefer 1 tablespoon of regular sugar, or 1/2" - Cubed size of Jaggery.

  5. Increase heat to high, and wait till boil.

  6. Once pot has achieved boil, add 2 cups of whole milk, or milk substitute.

  7. Wait till pot comes to a boil and milk rises to the top. However, do not turn off stove. Move the pot off of the burner to simmer.

  8. Put pot on burner again, and repeat previous step at least 4 times, or until Chai is golden brown in color.

  9. Pour chai through tea strainer into mugs, and serve. Enjoy your cup of deliciousness!

Additional Notes:

Chai, or better known as Masala Chai, is a mixed-spice tea combining black tea with milk. Origins of its inception are unknown, and many variants of chai exist, but origins stem to Assam tea in India, or "Mamri" tea.
